The problem

You don't have a
discipline problem.

Your phone was engineered to hijack your attention. You can't willpower your way out of architecture — trying harder is actually part of the trap.

The average person spends 4+ hours a day on their phone, most of it unintentional. The gap between unlocking and opening a feed is shorter than conscious thought. And habit apps? Most get deleted within two weeks because they just add guilt to the pile.

Ascent doesn't ask you to try harder. It redesigns what happens when you pick up your phone.

The Motivation Battery

A real-time readout of your dopamine receptor sensitivity — based on sleep, screen time, exercise, and what you've done today. It's not a vibe check. It's modeled on actual neuroscience. When the battery drops, Ascent nudges you before you burn out — not after.

AI coach that reads your data

It knows your battery level, your streaks, your Screen Time. When you're struggling, it doesn't say "you got this!" It says "do the 2-minute version and go to bed." Because that's what actually helps.

Screen Time, confronted

Ascent pulls your iOS Screen Time data directly. Not to shame you — to make the invisible visible. Hard to keep telling yourself "I don't scroll that much" when the number's right there next to your goals.

60-day curriculum, not a to-do list

You set the goal. AI generates a phased plan with weekly milestones that ramp up over time. Each morning you get one task — concrete, doable, connected to where you're headed. Not a wishlist you'll ignore by Wednesday.
